    About Me

    After graduating high school, I was a typical teen about to go into college wondering if the major I had chosen was right for me. I did not feel certain about it and was hoping I would find another career path as soon as possible. Luckily, a month before my first semester of college, I had been introduced to coding through my sister's friend: a self-taught programmer. His story fueled me to teach myself code and change my major to Computer Science. Even though I knew I could possibly learn to program in school, I did not want to wait around to embark on this journey of learning web development when I knew it was possible to get ahead and teach myself if I worked hard enough. So, I bought courses and other essentials in order to teach myself the core fundamentals of web development. I became hooked as soon as I saw the first lines of HTML load into the DOM and fell in love once the complexity was added with programming languages such as Javascript. I spent as much time as possible going through the courses, building projects, and striving to understand every single lesson no matter how difficult. I enjoyed continuously learning about new technologies and the feeling of triumph when figuring out the solution to a persisting problem, even if the problem was as miniscule as accidentally implementing the wrong type of brackets. I now have an understanding of the skills below and I hope to continue to expand my knowledge of other languages and of the best practices when it comes to web development.
