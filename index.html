    About Me

    After graduating high school, I was a typical teen about to go into college wondering if the major I had chosen, Kinesiology, was right for me. I did not feel certain about it and was hoping I would find another career path as soon as possible. Luckily, a month before my first semester of college, I had been introduced to coding through my sister's friend, a self-taught programmer. His story fueled me to teach myself code and change my major to Computer Science. Even though I knew I could possibly learn web development in school, I did not want to wait around to embark on this journey of being a software engineer when I knew it was possible to teach myself if I worked hard enough. So, I bought courses and other essentials in order to teach myself the core fundamentals of web development. I spent as much time as possible learning to code after school, in between classes, and sometimes during classes such as my CompSci 110 class :) . I became hooked as soon as I saw the first lines of HTML and fell in love once the complexity was added with programming languages such as Javascript. I enjoyed continuously learning about new technologies and the feeling of triumph when figuring out the solution to a persisting problem, even if the problem was as miniscule as implementing the wrong type of brackets. Now, I hope to continue to expand my knowledge of other languages and of the best practices when it comes to web development. I am currently looking for an opportunity to hone my skills in a professional setting and become a valuable asset to a team.
